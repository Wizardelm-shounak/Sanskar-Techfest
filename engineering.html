Engineering

Engineering

E in STEM stands for Engineering. It refers to the application of scientific knowledge to design, build, and create new products, structures, machines, and systems. Engineering involves using critical thinking, problem solving, and design skills to develop solutions to practical problems that have a real-world impact. Some examples of engineering fields include mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, civil engineering, and biomedical engineering. Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, development, and manufacturing of mechanical systems and devices, such as machines, engines, vehicles, and robots, using principles of mechanics, thermodynamics, and materials science. Mechanical engineers apply their knowledge of physics, mathematics, and engineering to solve problems related to the design, construction, and operation of mechanical systems.

Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace engineering is a specialized field of engineering that focuses on the design, development, testing, and maintenance of vehicles that operate in air or space. Aerospace engineers apply the principles of physics, mathematics, and materials science to solve problems related to the design and construction of aircraft, spacecraft, and missiles.

Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering

Civil engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of the built environment, including buildings, roads, bridges, tunnels, airports, water and sewage systems, and other infrastructure. Civil engineers apply their knowledge of mathematics, physics, and materials science to design and oversee construction projects, ensuring they are safe, efficient, and environmentally sustainable.
