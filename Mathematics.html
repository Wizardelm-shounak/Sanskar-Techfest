Mathematics

M in STEM stands for Mathematics. Mathematics is a fundamental discipline that provides the foundation for many other STEM fields. It involves the study of numbers, quantities, and shapes, and their relationships and operations. Mathematics is essential in fields such as engineering, physics, computer science, and economics, as it provides them with tools to model and solve complex problems. The use of mathematics has enabled humans to make advancements in technology, medicine, and research.

Mechanical engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, development, and manufacturing of mechanical systems and devices, such as machines, engines, vehicles, and robots, using principles of mechanics, thermodynamics, and materials science. Mechanical engineers apply their knowledge of physics, mathematics, and engineering to solve problems related to the design, construction, and operation of mechanical systems.

Algebra

Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of mathematical symbols and the rules for manipulating these symbols. It is a tool for solving mathematical problems that involve equations and unknown quantities.

Calculus

Calculus

Calculus is a branch of mathematics that deals with rates of change and the accumulation of small changes to determine a final result. It has two main branches: differential calculus and integral calculus.
